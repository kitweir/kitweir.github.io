Hi, I’m Kit. I’m a hobbyist chef, tattoo artist in training, and a UX designer. Travelling gives me anxiety, so I tend to stay at home on the unceded Musqueam, Tsleil-Waututh, and Squamish territories (Vancouver, BC). My practice is characterized by what I feel the world needs more of: sensitivity, care, and the consideration of often overlooked perspectives. I believe in questioning if our human-centered design is centering the right humans or not.

At YouTube, I helped viewers find desired content faster.

I spent my summer in California working on multiple projects to accelerate user journeys across various surfaces and entry points. I was responsible for collaborating with multiple stakeholders across Google & YouTube (including PM/UXR/Eng), creating prototypes for user testing in local and international markets, and presenting work to leadership.

Although I can’t speak to my work in detail, you can read about my experience here.

At Microsoft, my team and I designed, developed, and shipped the Read My World app in just 16 weeks.

As the sole designer on the team, I designed the project’s overall creative vision, including its visual identity, wireframes, mocks, prototypes, interaction design, project pitches, and weekly slide decks.

TechCrunch wrote an article about it, and I’ve also written up a walkthrough of the project here.
